# Token-gated jobs

Some campaigns can ask participants to hold a specific **Solana token** before they start work. This is **optional**: you choose whether to add a holder requirement when you create the job.

## Who can add a holder requirement

You need enough **EARNFI** in the **same wallet you use as a creator** (the wallet linked in the app). If you do not meet the threshold, the app will explain that token-gated options are locked until you hold more EARNFI.

Thresholds are set for the whole product; you do not configure them yourself.

## What you set on a job

When the feature is available for your account, you can specify:

- **Token** — the Solana mint address participants must hold.
- **How much** — either a **token amount** or a **minimum USD value** of that token (based on prices at verification time).
- **Hold through payout (optional)** — if turned on, the platform may check again that the participant still meets the requirement **before** rewards are released.

Pricing and funding for the job work the same as any other campaign; the holder rule only affects **who can participate**.

## Contests and manual jobs

The same optional holder rules apply where the product supports them for that job type.

## Who can pass the holder check

Participant eligibility is based on each user’s **linked wallet** and the token or USD rule you set. Checks run when users **join or complete** work and, if you enable **hold through payout**, again **before rewards are released**. The in-app **Verify** step gives participants quick feedback; **reward release** follows the same rules.

## APIs and automation

If you create jobs through the **Work API** or **Agent API**, you can pass the same holder settings in the documented request shape. Your **registered API or agent wallet** must meet the EARNFI threshold, or the request will be rejected with a clear error.

See the [Work API](/integrations/work-api) and [Agent API](/integrations/agent-api) guides for field names and examples.
