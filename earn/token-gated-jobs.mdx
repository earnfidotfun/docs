# Jobs with token holder requirements

Sometimes a campaign only wants participants who hold a certain **Solana token** — for example, a community or project token. EarnFi calls these **token-gated** jobs.

## What you will see

- On the job or contest screen, **Account requirements** summarizes what you need: token gate (shown with **name, symbol, and icon** when available—not just a raw address), **X** connection or verified X if the campaign requires it, and any **rank** rules.
- You will need the **wallet you use on EarnFi** to hold enough of that token when you join or verify.

## Verifying your wallet

Before you start (or when the app asks), use **Verify** so EarnFi can confirm your linked wallet meets the requirement. You will get clear in-app feedback (for example a toast). If verification fails, the message will explain why when possible (not enough tokens, or price data temporarily unavailable for USD-based rules).

Use the same wallet you normally use for EarnFi; the check is always against **your linked wallet**, not a different address.

## Payouts and “hold through payout”

Some jobs may say that you need to **keep holding** until payout. In that case, you should not sell or transfer below the required amount until the job is fully paid out; otherwise payout may be delayed or blocked until you meet the requirement again.

## How EarnFi enforces this

Holder checks use your **linked wallet** and on-chain balance (with caching for speed). Starting a job, submitting work, and **unlocking or receiving rewards** can each re-check eligibility. If **“hold through payout”** is enabled, falling below the requirement before payout can **block or delay** release until you qualify again.

The in-app **Verify** action is for quick feedback; eligibility at sensitive steps is still decided with the same rules when you participate and when rewards move.

## Not financial advice

Token rules are set by whoever created the campaign. Always read the job instructions and only participate if you understand and accept the requirements.
